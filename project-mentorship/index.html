Project-Based Mentorship

What does it mean to have a project-based experience and work with Albert as a mentor?

Project-based mentorship gives a learner the structure and guidance to turn an idea into tangible work, whether that means starting a nonprofit, building software, or launching a community organization. Along the way, learners build the kind of initiative and follow-through that helps them stand out on college applications.

Case Study: From Interest to Impact

An El Cerrito high school junior began one-on-one summer mentorship with weekly meetings. They started with a broad interest in education technology but no real direction.

Through guided conversations, we identified a specific problem worth solving, scoped a realistic first version, created digital mockups, and developed a finished case study. The result was a completed project with supporting materials, and it shaped the learner's decision to pursue Computer Science at UC Berkeley.

What a learner builds

  • Problem definition and scoping
  • Planning and communicating a first version (an MVP)
  • Working through ambiguity with confidence
  • Presenting ideas clearly to others

Who this is a good fit for

This mentorship works best for a learner who:

  • Has an existing interest or curiosity to build on
  • Can commit to steady, weekly progress
  • Wants to create something real, not hypothetical
  • Benefits from structure and a guide to stay on track

How I support the work

I provide a clear planning structure, help shape real deliverables, and offer ongoing guidance throughout, including connections within my network where they can help move a project forward.
